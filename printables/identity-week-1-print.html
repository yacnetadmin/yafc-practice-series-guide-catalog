Week One: The Journey

"There is only one problem on which all my existence, my peace, and my happiness depend; to discover myself in discovering God. If I find Him I will find myself and if I find my true self I will find Him." Thomas Merton

One of the keys to our discipleship in the way of Jesus is discovering and engaging our identity and calling. However, for many of us, the identity that Jesus has given us and how we should live in light of that doesn't largely play into our apprenticeship to Jesus. Instead, we often spend years finding out who our families say that we are, who our friends and co-worker say that we are, or even who the culture as a whole says that we should be. In the end, we end up failing at being someone we're not rather than succeeding as the person God created us to be.

Discovering and engaging our identity and calling is a process, not a one-time event. That was true for Jesus, and it will be true for us as well. No matter where you are in the journey with Him, it's not too late to start the journey!

Individual Practice

Set aside at least 30-60 minutes this week to reflect on your identity journey thus far.

  • First, reflect on those areas in which you've had any kind of clarity regarding identity and calling: These could be deep desires as a child or young adult, words of affirmation from others, a clear sense of what you wanted to do, and even words/ideas which you sense to be from God.
  • Then, make a list of "big dreams" that you have for your life—a business you'd like to start, a problem you'd like to solve, an impact that you'd like to have on society, etc.
  • Finally, take some time quiet to listen to the voice of the Holy Spirit speaking to your heart. What do you sense He is saying about you? Ask Him what identity He has given you. Consider how what you hear from Him connects with the lists you've made above.
