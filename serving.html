Introduction

"We believe that God has given every believer in Jesus at least one gift to be used for building up the church and for blessing the world... beyond the empowering and release of the gifts of the Spirit, there is simply a need to serve one another in basic ways within the body of Christ as Jesus did." York Alliance Family Values, Serving the Body

Serving one another seems to be the path to greatness in the way of Jesus. Over these four weeks, we will pursue serving as a key pathway to maturity in Christ.

Jesus repeatedly placed service at the center of kingdom life. From washing feet to teaching about greatness, he called his disciples to become servants of all.

"If I then, your Lord and Teacher, have washed your feet, you also ought to wash one another's feet." John 13:14-15
"Anyone who wants to be first must be the very last, and the servant of all." Mark 9:35

How to Use This Guide

The exercises in this booklet stretch over four weeks and align with Sunday teachings. You can find teachings at yorkalliance.org/sermons.

Transformation happens through biblical teaching, community, and practice. If you are not connected to a Community Group, you are encouraged to connect at yorkalliance.org/groups.

Recommended resources include The Active Life by Parker J. Palmer, Celebration of Discipline by Richard Foster, and Improving Your Serve by Charles Swindoll.

Week One: How to be Great

"Whoever would be great must be your servant." Matthew 20:26

Jesus reframes greatness through service. The path to the top in the kingdom begins and ends at the bottom.

Discuss in Community

Read Matthew 20:20-28 and discuss:

  • Why do you think Jesus references "the cup that I am to drink" in this moment?
  • How have you measured spiritual health, and how does that compare to Jesus' metric?
  • Do you desire to be a "great Christian"? Why?

Questions for Community

  1. What are your initial thoughts stepping into this service practice?
  2. What emotion surfaces when you think about living more deeply into service?
  3. Have you had someone meet a practical need for you, or done that for others?

Individual Practice

This week, intentionally serve someone already in your relational circle. Choose a specific act you would not naturally do otherwise, and do it quietly. Reflect on barriers, emotions, and how you sensed Jesus in the process.

Week Two: Gifts and Grace

"Don't think you are better than you really are." Romans 12:3 (NLT)

Everything is gift: breath, time, ability, personality, and spiritual gifting. Recognizing this helps us serve with humility rather than ego.

Discuss in Community

Read Romans 12:1-13 and discuss:

  • How do verses 3-13 connect with verses 1-2?
  • How does your group currently recognize that all members can participate in the Spirit's work?
  • What changes could help your meetings better reflect that reality?

Practice in Community

Walk through gifting discernment together: listen, reflect, identify strengths, and discern a practical way each person can test their gifting through service.

Individual Practice

Use SHAPE as a guide: Spiritual gifts, Heart, Abilities/talents, Personality, and Experiences. Note overlaps and identify your next faithful step of service.

Week Three: Growing Up in Christ

"People who know that they are embedded in an eternal community are both freed and empowered to become who they were born to be." Parker J. Palmer, The Active Life

There is no mature, isolated Christian life. Serving one another is not optional to growth; it is one of the central means by which we are formed into Christ's likeness.

Discuss in Community

Read Ephesians 4:11-16 and discuss:

  • What does it mean to equip the saints for ministry in your context?
  • When did you experience the greatest growth in your spiritual journey?
  • What does it practically mean that we belong to one another?

Questions for Community

  1. How are you currently serving the body, and do you sense joy and growth in it?
  2. Is there a way you might serve this group more intentionally?
  3. How willing are you to rearrange your schedule to meet needs?

Individual Practice

Assess your current service posture and commit to either a new regular area of ministry or one intentional unplanned act of service this week.

Week Four: Washing Feet

"As the cross is the sign of submission, so the towel is the sign of service." Richard J. Foster, Celebration of Discipline

Sometimes serving is not about gifting or preference. Sometimes serving is simply about a need in front of us and willingness to become the least.

Discuss in Community

Read John 13:1-17 and discuss:

  • How do you imagine you would have felt if you were in the room when Jesus washed feet?
  • What are practical needs you see regularly around you?
  • What might a modern-day equivalent of foot washing look like?

Questions for Community

  1. If you are not serving as much as you'd like, what gets in the way?
  2. How do you balance miracle-focused service and ordinary hidden service?
  3. How might you become more interruptible for spontaneous service?

Individual Practice

This week, intentionally serve someone outside your relational circle. Choose a concrete act you would not naturally do otherwise, and practice hidden service without drawing attention to yourself.
